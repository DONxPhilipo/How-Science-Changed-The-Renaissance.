Basic Information of the Renaissance period.

The Renaissance

Renaissance, which means "rebirth" in French, usually refers to the period of European history from 1400 to 1600 AD. However, many historians suggest that it started earlier or ended later, depending on the country. It bridges the ages of medieval and modern history and, depending on the country, overlaps with Early Modern, Elizabethan, and Restoration. The Renaissance is most closely associated with Italy, where it began in the 14th century, although countries such as Germany, Great Britain, and France have experienced many of the same phenomena and cultural changes. "Renaissance" comes from the French word for rebirth. According to Wikipedia-Renaissance, intense interest and study of antiquity "revived" after the Middle Ages, when classical philosophy was largely ignored or forgotten. Renaissance thinkers saw the Middle Ages as a period of cultural decline. They sought to revive their culture by re-emphasizing classical texts and philosophy. They expanded and interpreted them, creating their own styles of art, philosophy, and scientific research. Some of the major achievements of the Renaissance were astronomy, humanistic philosophy, printing press etc.

Fun Facts

What would be considered the most fascinating aspect of the Renaissance?

It was an incredible time of beauty, blossoming with creativity and curiosity. The Renaissance era also witnessed the discovery and exploration of new continents, the growth of commerce, and the inventions of innovations such as paper, printing, the mariner's compass and gunpowder.

What did scientists do in the Renaissance?

The Renaissance period experienced a surge in scientific exploration, with astronomy, anatomy, medicine, geography, alchemy, mathematics, and architecture all undergoing significant developments. One of the most notable Renaissance discoveries was made by Nicolaus Copernicus, a Polish astronomer and mathematician.

How did the Renaissance affect science and astronomy?

They say the sun and planets revolve around the earth. People have been taking this as fact. Astronomer Nicolaus Copernicus came up with a new theory during the Renaissance. He said that the sun is the center of the universe, and the earth and planets revolve around the sun.

Which Renaissance ideas carried over to the Scientific Revolution?

The Scientific Revolution was partly attributed to the rise of humanism during the Renaissance. At that time, artists and writers were deeply engrossed in exploring the natural world. This fascination with nature eventually seeped into the Scientific Revolution.

  • Gutenberg Press

    The printing press revolutionized society by standardizing language, spreading ideas, increasing literacy and developing the printing industry. The printing press was first invented in Europe by Johannes Gutenberg. It improved upon movable type printing, which appeared in Europe in the 15th century.

  • Vesalius drawing of a human skeleton

    Andreas Vesalius advanced the understanding of anatomy or the study of the human body. He created the first anatomy textbook, De humani corporis fabrica libri septem (The Seven Books on the Structure of the Human Body), to assist medical students in their research.

  • Harvey´s experiment on blood circulation

    Harvey is best known for his work on the circulation of blood. He discovered the function of the heart and the manner in which blood flows. This knowledge helps surgeons and enabled later progress on medicines.

  • Galileo´s telescope

    Galileo's discoveries with his telescope helped prove that the sun is the center of the solar system and not the earth. His observations strongly support a Sun-centered model, known as the heliocentric model, previously proposed by astronomers such as Nicolaus Copernicus.

  • flying machine

    Leonardo da Vinci conducted the first real study of flight in the 1480s. He had over 200 drawings and sketches illustrating his theory of flight. His Orthoplane, a plane that can fly by flapping its wings, developed a design to demonstrate how humans fly.

  • Pendulum clock

    Using mathematics and the fact that a pendulum rotates at a constant speed, Galileo was able to approximate the effect of gravity. These early experiments and the use of pendulums allowed scientists to calculate the shape of the Earth.
